    Mrs. Sherlock Holmes

    Overview

    I worked at Imminent XR as an intern in VP and VR developing my skills as a graphic/UX designer and learning Unity and Unreal Engine for virtual production and interactive games design. I contributed to various projects that explore a combination of VR, Virtual production and musical theatre.

    Project Details

    I created a 3D environment in Unreal Engine 5.2 for virtual production. The environment featured a blender model of the Reichenbach Falls and was used to tell the story of Mr. Sherlock Holmes’ death from Mrs. Sherlock Holmes’ perspective. Footage recorded in the virtual production studio was then chromakeyed and placed in the Unreal project as a 2D plane.

    Technical Skills

    3D modelling with Blender Using games engines, Unreal Engine 5.2 Creating 2D assets, Adobe Photoshop

    Developing the project

    Creating the first 3D models

    I started by creating the 3D models in Blender. I created a ‘rocky wall’ model for the surrounding environment and then also created a ‘cliff edge’.

    Assembling the 3D models in Unreal

    I reshaped the boulder models in Unreal to create a shape for the cliff face that spreads outwards over the falls. This platform would then be used for the 2D video plane of Mr. Sherlock Holmes.

    Adding the waterfall

    I used the cascade particle effect to create one waterfall and then layered the waterfall object at different parts of the rock wall model. This helped make better use of the space available in the environment and allowed me to add different directions for the water to follow.

    Live performance with virtual production

    Adding additional features

    I added the chromakeyed green screen studio footage of Mrs. Sherlock Holmes on a 2D flat plane I added ambient lighting features I added place holder objects of the battle to help judge the viewer perspective, the size of the 2D plane and its distance from the battle scene I added a virtual camera to compare the position of objects in the world space from the viewers perspective I added fog to simulate mist
